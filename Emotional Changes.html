"Blues"

Many parents experience negative feelings for a few weeks after birth

Common Symptoms Include: impatience, irritability, restlessness, anxiety, feeling tired, insomnia, sadness, mood changes, and poor concentration

Some Helpful Activities: Get plenty of rest, get out in nature, move around (walk, dance, etc), do things you love, make time for yourself

Postpartum Depression and Anxiety

Some parents experience moderate/severe depression and anxiety after the birth of their children.

Symptoms: The same as the “Blues”, but they may be more intense, felt more often, make it difficult to function, change your feelings toward your baby

Treatment: Consult a professional

Postpartum Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

This is a type of anxiety disorder that can happen after the birth of a baby.

Symptoms: Obsessions and compulsions that take up more than an hour a day (ex. Washing hands, repeatedly checking on the baby)

Treatment: Consult a professional

Postpartum Psychosis

This is a rare condition that can cause the parent to be dangerous to the baby. It requires immediate attention from a professional. Symptoms typically develop within 3-14 days after birth.

Symptoms vary widely but can include: Forgetting how to do things, having lots of energy/racing thoughts, having strange feelings, thoughts of self harm or harm to the baby, hallucinations, feeling afraid, agitation, and confusion

Treatment: Seek immediate help from a professional.
